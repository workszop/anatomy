The Four Layers

How Websites Are Built

Layer 1

Plain Text

Raw content — words, data, no formatting.

Analogy: Ingredients on a counter — nobody started cooking yet.
Layer 2

HTMLHyperText Markup Language — the skeleton giving structure to content.

Adds structure: headings, paragraphs, lists, images.

Analogy: Sorting ingredients into labelled bowls so the recipe works.
Layer 3

CSSCascading Style Sheets — the paint and furniture of your page.

Adds style: colors, fonts, spacing, layout.

Analogy: Decorating the cake — frosting, piping, arrangement.
Layer 4

JavaScriptA language that makes pages interactive — like a waiter who reacts.

Adds behaviour: click buttons, add to cart, live interactions.

Analogy: Hiring a waiter who takes orders and calculates the bill.
